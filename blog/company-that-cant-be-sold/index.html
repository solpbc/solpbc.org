the company that can't be sold

2026-03-25 · jer · 2 min read

most companies are built to be sold. sol pbc was built so it can’t be.

why this matters

when a company can be acquired, every decision is haunted by a question: “will this make us more attractive to buyers?” that question distorts everything — from product choices to data practices to how you treat the people who trust you.

we removed the question entirely. sol pbc’s articles of incorporation contain an irrevocable no-acquisition covenant. the company cannot be sold, merged, acquired, or reorganized. ever.

what this means in practice

  • your data stays yours. there’s no future acquirer who might “update the privacy policy” to monetize what you’ve shared.
  • the mission stays the mission. digital self-determination isn’t a marketing pitch we’ll abandon when the numbers don’t work. it’s a legal commitment.
  • the technology stays open. open source isn’t a growth hack. it’s the only way to build tools people can actually trust.

the structural trust thesis

trust isn’t a feature. it’s a structure.

the specific public benefit purpose of the Corporation is to advance digital self-determination by providing open-source, privacy-respecting tools and services that enable individuals to directly collect and gain insight from their personal data.

that’s not from a pitch deck. it’s from our articles of incorporation.

when the structure makes betrayal impossible, trust becomes a property of the system — not a promise from the people running it. people change. incentives change. structures, when built right, don’t.

building in the open

we’re sharing this because the ideas matter more than the competitive advantage. if more companies built structures that made trust inevitable rather than optional, the internet would be a fundamentally different place.

# this is what irrevocable means
class SolPBC:
    def acquire(self):
        raise ImpossibleError("nice try")

sol pbc exists to prove that technology companies can be built differently. not as a compromise, but as a better way.
