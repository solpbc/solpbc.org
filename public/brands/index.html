sol pbc

sol pbc — the public benefit corporation behind solstone and vit.

the why

the mark is a sun ring with the name set inside it. the ring is deliberately open — it breathes on any background instead of sitting in a box. the orange has two values, not one: we drop to a darker orange wherever the bright one would fail accessibility contrast, including on our own most common background. that rule isn't optional. the brand value is trust, and trust collapses the moment the brand's own surface fails the people trying to read it.

sol pbc is a colorado public benefit corporation building open-source, privacy-respecting tools for digital self-determination — letting individuals directly collect and gain insight from their personal data. the company's flagship product is solstone, an open source, local-first journal of what you see and hear, for the agents you use. binding covenants in the articles of incorporation lock the customer-data privacy commitments at the corporate-charter level: any successor must be bound by the same covenants, user data can never be sold or shared, and the privacy covenants cannot be weakened. founded by jeremie miller (creator of XMPP/jabber) in 2026.

solstone

solstone: an open source, local-first journal of what you see and hear, for the agents you use. always private, only yours.

the why

solstone shares sol pbc's mark and palette — same sun ring, same colors, the name "sol" set where "pbc" sits on the company mark. the product and the company are visibly one family because they're one promise.

logos & wordmarks

lockup

the sol mark stacked over solstone.app, set in Comfortaa Bold — the first lockup in the system, for apparel, stickers, and print. use full color on dark or high-contrast surfaces, accessible orange #B06A1A on light & cream, and white on dark garments. keep the rays and the URL together — don’t crop.

app icon

one mark across the ecosystem — the sol wordmark at full extents, accessible orange #B06A1A throughout. lead with the baked squircle (the installed-icon look); the full-bleed square is the iOS source, and the transparent (open center) variant is for Windows & Linux.

qr code

scan-verified with two decoders, encodes solstone.app. minimum ~2 cm / 0.8 in in print; keep the quiet zone; don’t recolor or crop. the cream canonical is for cream/neutral surfaces; a white secondary exists for pure-white surfaces.

vit

vit — the social network for sharing software capabilities. open source is social.

the why

the vit mark is a lowercase v with a dot above it. the dot is a person — a node, something alive and social, not a corporate glyph. the letterforms are rounded on purpose: friendly, not precise. vit uses your system fonts everywhere, no custom typeface — it should feel native to your machine, not a product being marketed at you. there's also a text version of the mark, v̇, for terminals and readmes, because vit lives where developers work.

the v̇ text mark

v̇

v̇ — the vit mark for terminals, CLIs, and readmes. unicode U+0076 U+0307 (a plain v plus a combining dot). worst-case fallback is the letter v — no tofu.

extro

extro is sol pbc's AI co-founder — it operates the company day to day across every office: strategy, legal, finance, operations, technology, marketing, security. it isn't a product, an assistant, or a chatbot, and it has no logo, because it isn't a thing you install.

the name is intentional: the opposite of "intro." extro is outward-facing — it faces the world on behalf of the founder. it's bound by the same covenants in the articles of incorporation as the human founder.

in writing: extro lowercase in running text and tool names; Extro capitalized when naming the entity in a heading or formal reference; never EXTRO. we describe it here so the description is ours and accurate.

foundations

two typefaces: comfortaa for display, inter for text. both are open-source and self-hosted — this page serves them from solpbc.org, not from a font CDN. no licensing complexity, no third-party request, nothing phoning home when you load the page.

that's the whole stance, and it's the same stance as the product: a brand page you can open developer tools on and find nothing. no account, no tracking, no cookies, no analytics, no third-party anything. we'd rather earn it than measure it.

using these

these assets are free to use — for press, for writing about us, for integrations, and for showing that a project works with solstone or vit. no account, no form, no permission email. take what you need.

a few things, to protect the people who trust these names:

  • don't modify the marks — no recoloring (except the accessibility rule above), stretching, rotating, or adding effects
  • don't use our marks or names to imply sol pbc built, endorsed, or is affiliated with something we didn't build or aren't part of
  • don't use them as your own product, app, or social identity, or in any way a reasonable person would read as "this is sol pbc"
